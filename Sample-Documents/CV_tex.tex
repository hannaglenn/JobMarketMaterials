          {\textbf{Does Hospital Leadership Matter? Evidence from Pay-for-Performance} }
          
          \vspace{-3mm}
              Firm leaders can be important drivers of culture, direction, and strategies of firms. \section{Working Papers}
    \vspace{-5pt}
    \resumeSubHeadingListStart
      \resumeProjectHeading
          {\textbf{Labor Markets and Technological Change: Evidence from EHRs $|$ \emph{R\&R at Journal of Health Economics} }}
          
            \vspace{-3mm}
          
          In this paper, I investigate changes in physician behavior as a result of a major technology shift in US hospitals, the adoption of electronic health records (EHRs). I treat EHR adoption in hospitals as an exogenous shock to hospitalists, and estimate average group time treatment effects on various labor market outcomes. I find that hospitalists are more likely to leave clinical work altogether, and more likely to shift work to an office setting due to EHRs. For hospitalists that continue working in the hospital, EHRs lead to an increase in patients seen. 

         \resumeSubHeadingListEnd
